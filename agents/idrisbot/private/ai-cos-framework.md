# FDE Engagement Framework

## Overview

**The product is the FDE engagement.** AI CoS is a feature of that engagement, not the product itself.

The FDE engagement starts with a **sprint** — a free assessment that gets your foot in the door. The sprint is the onramp. It earns the right to build. The build earns the right to deploy. The loop runs again.

This reframe was established 2026-07-22 when Jae corrected the CEO review framing: "AI CoS is just a feature of the FDE engagement. The FDE engagement starts with an assessment. Give it away for free. The point is to do interviews, get a feel for the client's world, identify what matters. Build the framework for the CoS from there."

### Source Language (Vas / Greg Isenberg)

> Greg: "We noticed that the word audit was a tough pill for people to swallow. And we just rebranded audit as a sprint."
> Vas: "For some reason, people have an allergic reaction to the word audit."

## The FDE Loop (Delivery Structure)

The 4-phase framework below is the **sales structure** (how you scope and sell the engagement). The **delivery structure** is the FDE loop: audit → build → evals → deploy → observe → improve → loop runs again.

"Each stage earns the right to the next." — Vas

### Phase 1: Sprint / Discovery (Free — Week 1-2)

**Goal:** Understand the client's current state, pain points, and strategic objectives. Map the real workflow — not the documented one.

**This is the free assessment.** Give it away. The point is to get your foot in the door, do the interviews, sit with the person, coax out the tribal knowledge, map the real workflow, and build the ROI matrix.

**Deliverables:**
- Stakeholder interview notes
- Operating map (current-state workflow — the real process, not the documented one)
- Pain point inventory (ranked by impact × feasibility)
- ROI matrix (what's worth automating vs. what isn't)
- Discovery report

**Forcing Questions:**
- What does the executive team obsess about at 2am?
- If we solved this problem, what would the team do with the freed-up time?
- What's the cost of NOT solving this?
- Who are the internal skeptics and what are their specific objections?
- What has been tried before and why did it fail?

**Selling the Sprint:**
- "The documented process is rarely the real process" — opens the conversation
- "The reality is in one person's head" — creates urgency
- "We map it out very cleanly" — promises clarity
- "You keep the map regardless" — removes risk
- "The audit was worth 10x what they paid" — social proof
- "Better than McKinsey because it's so telling" — competitive positioning

### Phase 2: Foundation / Quick Wins (Week 2-4)

**Goal:** Deliver tangible value fast. Deploy quick wins while designing the broader AI CoS architecture.

**Quick Wins:**
- Second brain (knowledge capture and retrieval system)
- Personal agent infrastructure (the client's first AI tools)

**Architecture Deliverables:**
- AI CoS architecture document
- Agent role definitions
- Data pipeline design
- KPI dashboard mockup
- Governance and security framework
- Implementation roadmap

**Forcing Questions:**
- What data exists today vs. what we wish existed?
- Which agent role, if built first, would create the most visible value?
- What's the smallest thing we can deploy that makes someone say "wow"?
- How will we measure success? (Leading vs. lagging indicators)
- What happens when the AI is wrong? (Fallback, escalation, human-in-the-loop)

### Phase 3: Deployment / AI CoS (Week 4-8)

**Goal:** Build, test, and deploy the AI CoS — the ongoing intelligence system built from what the sprint revealed.

**Deliverables:**
- Working AI agents in production
- Integrated data pipelines
- KPI dashboard live
- User training materials
- Documentation and handoff guides
- Evidence log (every step recorded — non-negotiable)

**Forcing Questions:**
- Are we building technology or creating outcomes? (Outcomes win)
- Who on the client team is becoming the internal champion?
- What's the first moment of truth — when will the client see value?
- What are we NOT building that the client expects? (Manage expectations)

**Deployment Progression:**
Sandbox (full human review) → Shadow Mode (human reviews every output) → Gradual Autonomy (human approves specific types) → Production (human reviews on exception only)

### Phase 4: The Loop / Ongoing (Week 8+)

**Goal:** The engagement doesn't end — it deepens. Each cycle reveals the next bottleneck.

**Deliverables:**
- Complete documentation
- Runbooks for common operations
- Training completion certificates
- Support tier agreement
- 30/60/90-day check-in schedule
- Next-cycle operating map (the loop runs again)

**Forcing Questions:**
- Can the client team operate this without us? (If no, we're not done)
- What's the maintenance burden and who owns it?
- What's the expansion path? (Next agents, next data sources, next use cases)
- How do we turn this engagement into a referenceable case study?
- What's the next bottleneck the loop revealed?

## Pricing Model

**Sprint (Phase 1):** Free. Give it away. Foot in the door.
**Foundation + Deployment (Phases 2-3):** Value-based project fee. "Only get paid when you really prove measurable value." — Vas
**The Loop (Phase 4+):** Monthly retainer. Ongoing FDE loop — each cycle reveals the next bottleneck.

_First 2-3 clients are education: "They are genuinely worth more to you than you are to them." — Vas. After 2-3, you have leverage — charge because you have proof._

## Key Patterns

- **The sprint is the onramp, not the product** — give it away, earn the right to build
- **The documented process is rarely the real process** — audit before building
- **Build on existing systems** — don't force migrations. Domo is the wedge, not the ceiling.
- **Audit trail is non-negotiable** — clients need to see what the agent is doing
- **Human in the loop for approval** — one clear decision point
- **Each stage earns the right to the next** — sprint earns build, build earns deploy, deploy earns expand
- **The loop runs again** — every workflow improvement reveals the next bottleneck
- **Start with the smallest useful action** — increase autonomy gradually
- **You're not selling AI, you're selling a promotion** — help them get promoted, not replaced
- **Document everything** — it becomes the next engagement's sales material
